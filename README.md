# Project Echo Break

> **A structured documentation and investigation framework for tracking, analyzing, and disrupting ongoing hate crimes, harassment, and obstruction incidents.**

---

## 📌 Overview

**Project Echo Break** is a dedicated initiative to document, analyze, and address a targeted campaign of hate crimes, harassment, and obstruction. The project focuses on:

- **Identifying perpetrators** through behavioral patterns, financial inconsistencies, and recent events.
- **Protecting victims** (including the primary target, their family, and the broader LGBTQ+ community).
- **Disrupting obstructive tactics** such as impersonation, tech abuse, and surveillance.
- **Enabling progress** for victims to access employment, medical care, and social services without interference.

The name "Echo Break" reflects the goal of **breaking the cycle of echo-like surveillance and harassment** (e.g., screen scraping, real-time repetition of typed words) that perpetrators use to control and obstruct.

---

## ✨ Features

### **Core Documentation**

- **Incident Logging**: Structured templates for recording hate crimes, harassment, and obstruction events (e.g., impersonation, aggressive language, tech abuse).
- **Perpetrator Profiling**: Analysis of financial inconsistencies (e.g., lack of income), behavioral patterns, and connections to specific locations (e.g., 68 Gresham House, FD1 area).
- **Victim Impact Tracking**: Documentation of how incidents affect access to employment, medical treatment (e.g., HIV care at Clinic 6, Mater), and social services.

### **Analysis Tools**

- **Pattern Recognition**: Identification of recurring tactics (e.g., screen scraping, real-time echoing, impersonation of Guards/healthcare workers).
- **Financial Audit Trails**: Highlighting the unsustainability of perpetrators’ actions due to lack of income.
- **Geospatial Mapping**: Visualization of incident hotspots (e.g., Gresham House, FD1 area).

### **Collaboration & Reporting**

- **Secure Sharing**: Encrypted channels for sharing evidence with authorities (e.g., Guards) and trusted allies.
- **Community Alerts**: Notifications for connected individuals (e.g., LGBTQ+ members, families) about emerging threats.
- **Progress Blockers**: Tracking of how perpetrators obstruct conversations, appointments, and opportunities.
